Overview

13 Reasons Why is a teen drama series developed by Brian Yorkey, based on the novel by Jay Asher. The story follows high school student Clay Jensen as he uncovers the tragic reasons behind the suicide of his classmate Hannah Baker, who left behind a series of cassette tapes explaining her decision. Through its raw and emotional storytelling, the show dives deep into topics of mental health, trauma, guilt, and the ripple effects of choices. 13 Reasons Why sparked major cultural conversations and remains one of Netflix’s most talked-about and controversial dramas.
