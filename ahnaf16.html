Overview

Oppenheimer is a biographical drama directed by Christopher Nolan that chronicles the life of physicist J. Robert Oppenheimer, the father of the atomic bomb.
The film explores his complex moral struggles, the scientific breakthroughs during World War II, and the profound impact of nuclear weapons on humanity.
Featuring intense performances and Nolan’s signature storytelling, Oppenheimer is a gripping portrayal of history and human ambition.
