Overview

Breaking Bad is a critically acclaimed American TV series created by Vince Gilligan. It follows Walter White, a struggling high school chemistry teacher diagnosed with lung cancer, who turns to manufacturing methamphetamine with his former student Jesse Pinkman. What begins as a desperate attempt to secure his family's future spirals into a criminal empire, transforming Walter into the ruthless drug lord known as "Heisenberg." The show explores morality, power, crime, and the consequences of choices.
