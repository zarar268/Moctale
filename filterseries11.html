Overview

Bojack Horseman is a critically acclaimed animated dramedy created by Raphael Bob-Waksberg. Set in a world where humans and anthropomorphic animals coexist, the show follows Bojack — a washed-up 90s sitcom star struggling with addiction, depression, fame, and self-destruction. Through sharp writing, surreal humor, and emotional honesty, Bojack Horseman delivers a powerful exploration of mental health, identity, and redemption in Hollywood. It’s both hilarious and heartbreaking — a rare blend of satire and sincerity that redefined adult animation.
