Overview

Fight Club is a psychological thriller film directed by David Fincher, based on Chuck Palahniuk’s novel. It follows the story of an unnamed narrator who creates an underground fight club as a way to escape his dull and consumer-driven life. The club grows into a dangerous movement led by the mysterious and charismatic Tyler Durden. It explores themes of identity, masculinity, mental health, and society’s obsession with materialism. The film is known for its dark tone, plot twists, and thought-provoking message. Today, Fight Club is considered a cult classic that challenges viewers to question reality and themselves.
